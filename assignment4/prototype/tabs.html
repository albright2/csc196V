Histogram

This is a prototype of what the histogram for my dataset would look like.

I am using a histogram to either show a countries fertility rate and/or life expectancy over time.

It is still a work in progress. So far the output works for the example dataset.

The purpose is to see the trend in fertility rates, population, and life span.

I am hoping to include a comparison between countries as well.

I imagine the final product to include a drop down menu where you are able to select one or more countries to compare.

The counties will then be plotted on the same visualiztion, so that it will be easier to track the changes/differences between the countries as time passes.

Sunburst Direct graph

source = https://bl.ocks.org/kerryrodden/7090426

This page will have a drop-down menu list with selectable years that will update the sunburst graph.

Once a year has been chosen, between the year 1960-2016, the sunburst graph will be similar to a pi-chart with countries representing a porportion of the total population.

To explore the sunburst, start from the middle where "100%" is displayed (100% of total population), and navigate outwards from continent and then to country.

The overall goal is to compare country populations within a given year, in a easy to read and fun to explore format.

This graph will best solve the question of which countries have the most population within a said year, and perhaps teach someone which countries belong to what continent.

Maximum and Minimium Charts

The purpose of these visualiztion is to compare the 5 more poplated to the 5 least populated countries for specfic years.

The final product would include a drop down menu, or a scroll bar, where the user will select the year, and it would display the top five and lowest five countries.

I would also like to include buttons for all three values (population, life span, and fertility); that way the user will also be able to decide which values to observe.

The question that I hope to answer is to understand which countries excel in these three areas, and see what or how they are different from those countries that struggle in these areas.
